Orthopedics

ORTHOPEDIC
SERVICES

Expert care for bones, joints, muscles, and the musculoskeletal system — from sports injuries to complex joint reconstruction.

Musculoskeletal conditions are the leading cause of disability worldwide. Our orthopedic team offers both surgical and non-surgical options, always prioritising the least invasive approach that delivers the best long-term outcome for each patient.

About Our Orthopedic Department

Our Orthopedic Department combines the expertise of subspecialty surgeons with a dedicated physiotherapy and rehabilitation team to deliver complete musculoskeletal care. Whether you are recovering from a sports injury, managing chronic joint pain, or requiring complex reconstruction surgery, our team tailors every treatment plan to your specific needs and lifestyle goals.

Conditions We Treat

  • Fractures, dislocations, and traumatic bone injuries
  • Osteoarthritis and degenerative joint disease
  • Sports injuries: ligament tears, tendon ruptures, meniscal damage
  • Spine conditions: herniated discs, spinal stenosis, scoliosis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis and inflammatory joint disease
  • Osteoporosis and fragility fractures
  • Paediatric orthopaedic conditions: club foot, growth plate injuries
  • Bone tumours and infections (osteomyelitis)

Treatments & Procedures

Joint Replacement

Total hip, knee, and shoulder replacement using the latest implant technology for lasting pain relief.

Arthroscopy

Minimally invasive keyhole surgery of the knee, shoulder, and hip for diagnosis and treatment.

Fracture Fixation

Surgical fixation of complex fractures using plates, screws, nails, and external fixators.

Sports Medicine

ACL reconstruction, rotator cuff repair, and return-to-sport rehabilitation programs.

Spinal Surgery

Discectomy, spinal fusion, and decompression procedures for painful spinal conditions.

Non-Surgical Care

Physiotherapy, cortisone injections, PRP therapy, and bracing for non-operative management.

Why Choose Our Orthopedic Unit

  • Fellowship-Trained Surgeons: Each surgeon has subspecialty training in their specific area — knee, hip, spine, or sports medicine.
  • Robotic-Assisted Surgery: Robotic arm technology for highly precise joint replacement with optimal implant positioning.
  • Rapid Fracture Clinic: Same-day assessment and management of acute fractures and injuries.
  • Physiotherapy Integration: In-house rehabilitation starts immediately post-surgery for faster recovery.
  • Sports Injury Programme: Dedicated pathway for athletes with fast-track assessment and return-to-sport planning.
  • 3D Pre-Surgical Planning: CT-based templating and 3D printing used for complex reconstructive cases.
  • Pain Management Team: Joint access to pain specialists for patients with chronic musculoskeletal pain.
