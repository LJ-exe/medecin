Comprehensive diagnosis and treatment of disorders affecting the brain, spinal cord, and nervous system — delivered by our expert neurological team.

Neurological disorders affect over 1 billion people worldwide. Early diagnosis is critical — our team uses the most advanced imaging and neurophysiology tools to detect conditions at their earliest, most treatable stage.

About Our Neurology Department

Our Neurology Department is staffed by board-certified neurologists with subspecialty expertise in epilepsy, stroke, movement disorders, and neuromuscular disease. We combine cutting-edge diagnostics with compassionate patient care to manage even the most complex neurological conditions. Our clinic offers both outpatient consultations and inpatient neurological care.

Conditions We Treat

Our neurologists are trained to diagnose and manage a wide range of brain and nervous system disorders, including:

  • Stroke and transient ischemic attack (TIA)
  • Epilepsy and seizure disorders
  • Parkinson's disease and movement disorders
  • Multiple sclerosis (MS) and demyelinating diseases
  • Migraine, cluster headaches, and chronic headache syndromes
  • Dementia and Alzheimer's disease
  • Peripheral neuropathy and nerve damage
  • Brain and spinal cord tumours (co-managed with neurosurgery)

Diagnostic Tools & Procedures

Our neurodiagnostic unit is equipped with the latest technology for precise, non-invasive assessment:

MRI Brain & Spine

High-resolution magnetic resonance imaging for detailed visualisation of brain and spinal cord structures.

EEG

Electroencephalography to record brain electrical activity and diagnose epilepsy and sleep disorders.

Nerve Conduction Studies

Electromyography and nerve conduction tests to assess peripheral nerve and muscle function.

CT Brain Scan

Rapid CT imaging essential in stroke and head trauma workups to detect bleeds and lesions.

Lumbar Puncture

Cerebrospinal fluid analysis for diagnosing meningitis, MS, and other neurological conditions.

Visual Evoked Potentials

Measures the brain's electrical response to visual stimulation — key in diagnosing optic nerve disease.
